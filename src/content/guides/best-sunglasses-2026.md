## How to Choose Sunglasses

**Polarized lenses matter more than frame style.** Polarization cuts glare from reflective surfaces like water, roads, and car windshields — a genuine functional benefit beyond fashion, and worth prioritizing whether you're driving, at the beach, or just walking around a sunny city.

**Face shape guides frame shape, not the other way around.** Aviators and rounder frames tend to soften angular faces, while cat eye and oversized square shapes add definition to rounder face shapes. There's no universally "best" shape — it depends on what you're balancing.

**UV400 protection should be non-negotiable regardless of price.** This standard blocks 99-100% of UVA and UVB rays, and it's now common even in budget sunglasses — always confirm it's listed before buying, since cheap sunglasses without real UV protection can actually be worse for your eyes than no sunglasses at all (dilated pupils let in more unfiltered light).

**Buying multiple affordable pairs beats one expensive pair for most people.** At under $15 each, having a couple of different styles for different outfits and occasions costs less than a single premium designer pair, without meaningfully sacrificing protection quality.
