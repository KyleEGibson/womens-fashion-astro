## How to Build a Well-Rounded Shoe Wardrobe

**One good pair per category beats several mediocre pairs in one category.** Rather than owning five pairs of sneakers, most people are better served having one solid option each for exercise, everyday comfort, warm weather, cooler weather, and dressier occasions.

**Arch support matters most in flats and sandals.** Because these styles lack the cushioning structure sneakers naturally have, look specifically for flats and sandals marketed with arch support if you're on your feet for extended periods — it's the difference between all-day comfort and foot fatigue by early afternoon.

**Heel height should match your actual tolerance, not aspiration.** A lower, more comfortable heel you'll actually wear all night beats a higher statement heel that gets kicked off under the table after an hour.

**Boots are worth buying seasonally appropriate.** A waterproof ankle boot serves a genuinely different purpose than a western-style boot with a chunky heel — decide whether you need weather protection or a style statement before buying.

## Our Recommendation

Build out your shoe wardrobe by category rather than chasing trends in just one: the adidas running shoe for workouts, CUSHIONAIRE flats for daily comfort, Goosecret sandals for summer, IUV boots for a trendy cool-weather look, and IDIFU pumps for dressier occasions.
